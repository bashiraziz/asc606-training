# Module 8 — Other key topics

## Contract costs — ASC 340-40

ASC 340-40 governs the accounting for costs to obtain and fulfill contracts with customers. These rules run alongside ASC 606 and affect both the income statement and the balance sheet.

### Costs to obtain a contract

Costs that would not have been incurred if the contract had not been obtained — typically **sales commissions**.

| Treatment | Condition |
|---|---|
| **Capitalize** as contract cost asset | Incremental costs expected to be recovered |
| **Expense as incurred** (practical expedient) | Amortization period would be ≤1 year |

The capitalized asset is amortized on a **systematic basis consistent with the transfer of the related goods/services**.

**Amortization period:** The expected contract term, including expected renewals if the commission is not paid again on renewal.

### Costs to fulfill a contract

Costs incurred to fulfill a contract that do not qualify under another standard (e.g., inventory, fixed assets) are capitalized if **all three** criteria are met:

1. The costs **relate directly** to a specific contract (or anticipated contract)
2. The costs **generate or enhance resources** that will be used to satisfy POs
3. The costs are **expected to be recovered**

**Common examples of capitalizable fulfillment costs:**
- Setup and mobilization costs for a long-term service contract
- Pre-contract engineering costs for a contract clearly expected to be signed
- Direct labor costs to set up a customer environment in a SaaS implementation

**Costs that cannot be capitalized:** General and administrative costs, wasted materials, costs that relate to satisfied POs.

### Impairment

Recognize impairment loss when the carrying amount of the contract cost asset exceeds the remaining expected recoverable amount (revenue remaining − directly related costs still to be incurred).

## Licenses of intellectual property

Licenses of IP are common in software, media, pharma, and franchise arrangements. The accounting depends on the **nature of the license**.

### Two types of licenses (ASC 606-10-55-58)

| Type | Description | Recognition |
|---|---|---|
| **Right to access** | License gives access to the entity's IP throughout the license period; entity continues to maintain/develop the IP | **Over time** |
| **Right to use** | License provides a right to use the entity's IP as it exists at the point in time when granted | **Point in time** |

### Determining the type

A license is a right-to-access (over-time) if **all three** criteria are met:
1. The contract requires or the customer reasonably expects that the entity will undertake activities that significantly affect the IP
2. Those activities expose the customer directly to the effects (positive or negative)
3. Those activities do not result in the transfer of a good or service to the customer

If any criterion is not met → right-to-use (point in time).

**Examples:**

| Scenario | Type | Recognition |
|---|---|---|
| Movie streaming subscription | Right to access | Over time |
| Perpetual software license (static version) | Right to use | Point in time |
| Drug compound license with ongoing R&D support | Right to access | Over time |
| Music album download | Right to use | Point in time |
| Franchise license with ongoing brand support | Right to access | Over time |

### Sales-based and usage-based royalties

An exception applies to royalties based on sales or usage of IP: recognize revenue **only when (or as) the sale or usage occurs** (ASC 606-10-55-65). This is true even if the allocation criteria for variable consideration would otherwise require earlier recognition.

## Warranties

Warranties are promises to repair or replace defective products. The accounting depends on the **type of warranty**.

### Assurance-type warranties

A warranty that only assures the product will function as intended — i.e., it meets agreed-upon specifications.

**Accounting:** Account for under ASC 460 (Guarantees) as a cost accrual — NOT a separate performance obligation.

### Service-type warranties

A warranty that provides a service **beyond** assuring the product functions as intended — for example, a warranty that covers accidental damage or includes proactive maintenance.

**Accounting:** A separate **performance obligation** — allocate a portion of the transaction price to the warranty and recognize over the warranty period.

### How to distinguish

| Question | Assurance-type | Service-type |
|---|---|---|
| Is the warranty required by law? | Often yes | Rarely |
| Is the warranty sold separately? | Usually no | Often yes |
| Does it extend beyond defect repair? | No | Yes |
| Is it for a longer period than industry standard? | No | Often yes |

## Material rights — customer options

A customer option to acquire additional goods or services is a **performance obligation** (material right) if it provides a discount that the customer would not receive without the contract — i.e., a significant discount relative to the standalone price.

**Accounting:** Allocate a portion of the transaction price to the option. Recognize when:
- The option is exercised (additional goods/services are transferred), or
- The option expires unexercised

**Estimating SSP of the material right:**
- SSP = probability-weighted estimate of goods/services expected to be acquired × discount per unit

**Practical alternative:** An entity may estimate the standalone selling price of the option directly if it can reasonably estimate it.

## Key takeaways

- Contract costs (ASC 340-40): capitalize incremental costs to obtain (commissions) and direct costs to fulfill if expected to be recovered; amortize over the expected benefit period; practical expedient for ≤1-year contracts
- License type determines timing: right-to-access = over time; right-to-use = point in time; apply the three-part test
- Sales-based royalties are recognized only when the underlying sale/usage occurs — no earlier
- Assurance warranties → cost accrual under ASC 460; service-type warranties → separate PO under ASC 606
- Material rights (discounted options) are separate POs — allocate transaction price and recognize on exercise or expiry
