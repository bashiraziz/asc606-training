# Module 7 — Step 5: Recognize revenue

## The recognition principle

Revenue is recognized when (or as) the entity **satisfies a performance obligation** by transferring a promised good or service to the customer (ASC 606-10-25-23). Transfer occurs when the customer obtains **control**.

**Control** means the ability to direct the use of, and obtain substantially all of the remaining benefits from, the asset.

## Over-time recognition (ASC 606-10-25-27)

A performance obligation is satisfied **over time** if any one of three criteria is met:

### Criterion 1 — Customer simultaneously receives and consumes benefits

The customer receives and consumes the benefits of the entity's performance as it performs.

**Examples:** Routine cleaning services, transaction processing, payroll processing — each period's service is consumed as delivered.

### Criterion 2 — Entity creates or enhances a customer-controlled asset

The entity's performance creates or enhances an asset that the **customer controls** as the entity performs.

**Examples:** Custom construction on customer-owned land, software development on a customer-hosted platform, improvements to customer-owned property.

### Criterion 3 — No alternative use + enforceable right to payment

The entity's performance does not create an asset with an **alternative use** to the entity, AND the entity has an **enforceable right to payment** for performance completed to date (including a reasonable profit margin).

**Examples:** Highly customized manufactured goods built to customer specifications that cannot be redirected; long-term service contracts with pay-for-performance clauses.

:::warning Both parts of Criterion 3 must be met
If the asset has no alternative use but the entity cannot enforce payment for work done to date, over-time recognition is not permitted.
:::

## Point-in-time recognition

If none of the over-time criteria are met, revenue is recognized at a **single point in time** when control transfers to the customer.

Indicators of control transfer at a point in time (ASC 606-10-25-30):

| Indicator | Notes |
|---|---|
| Present right to payment | Customer is obligated to pay |
| Legal title has transferred | Possession or title passed |
| Physical possession | Customer holds the asset |
| Risks and rewards of ownership | Customer bears loss risk |
| Customer accepted | Formal acceptance or implied by conduct |

No single indicator is determinative — evaluate all facts and circumstances.

## Measuring progress for over-time obligations

When a PO is satisfied over time, an **appropriate method** must be selected to measure progress (ASC 606-10-25-31). The method must be applied **consistently** to similar POs.

### Output methods

Measure progress based on direct measures of value to the customer:
- Units produced or delivered
- Milestones reached
- Survey of performance completed

**Best when:** Output can be directly observed and reliably measured.

### Input methods

Measure progress based on effort or resources consumed relative to total expected inputs:
- Costs incurred vs. total expected costs (most common for construction)
- Labor hours incurred vs. total expected hours
- Machine hours consumed

**Best when:** A direct relationship exists between inputs consumed and value transferred.

:::tip Cost-to-cost method
The cost-to-cost input method is the most common for long-term contracts:

`Progress % = Costs incurred to date ÷ Total estimated contract costs`

`Revenue recognized = Progress % × Total transaction price − Prior cumulative revenue`

Update estimates at every reporting date. Cost overruns → reduce future revenue. Cost savings → increase future revenue.
:::

:::warning Uninstalled materials exception
Costs incurred for materials not yet incorporated into the work (e.g., materials delivered to site but not installed) should be excluded from the cost-to-cost calculation — they don't reflect progress.
:::

## Bill-and-hold arrangements (ASC 606-10-55-81)

A bill-and-hold arrangement is one where the entity bills the customer but retains physical possession of the product at the customer's request.

Revenue may be recognized before physical delivery only if **all four** criteria are met:

1. The reason for the bill-and-hold is **substantive** (e.g., the customer does not yet have capacity to receive)
2. The product is **separately identified** as belonging to the customer
3. The product is **ready for physical transfer** to the customer
4. The entity does **not have the ability to use the product** or to redirect it to another customer

:::danger All four required
Missing even one criterion means bill-and-hold recognition is not permitted. The most commonly failed criterion is #4 — entities that commingle bill-and-hold inventory with their own cannot recognize revenue.
:::

## Consignment arrangements (ASC 606-10-55-79)

In a consignment arrangement, the entity delivers products to a dealer/distributor but retains control until a specified event occurs (typically end-customer sale).

**Recognition rule:** Do not recognize revenue when products are placed on consignment. Recognize only when:
- The consignee sells the product to an end customer, or
- Another event occurs that transfers control per the agreement (e.g., consignment period expires)

**Indicators of consignment:**
- Product is held by another party for sale
- Entity can require return or transfer to another party
- Consignee does not have unconditional obligation to pay

## Key takeaways

- Revenue is recognized when (or as) control of the promised good or service transfers — over time or at a point in time
- Over-time recognition requires meeting **one** of three criteria: simultaneous consumption, customer-controlled asset creation, or no alternative use + payment right
- Progress for over-time POs is measured using output or input methods — select and apply consistently
- Bill-and-hold requires all four criteria to be met — substantive reason, separate identification, ready for transfer, no redirect ability
- Consignment revenue is deferred until the consignee sells to an end customer — placing goods on consignment does not transfer control
