# Module 10 — Case studies & knowledge check

## Case study 1 — SaaS contract with onboarding and variable bonus

### Facts

TechCo signs a 24-month SaaS agreement with a large enterprise customer. The contract includes:

- **SaaS platform access** — $8,000/month for 24 months = $192,000 base
- **Onboarding and data migration** — a fixed-fee service completed in month 1
- **Performance bonus** — $24,000 payable if uptime ≥ 99.9% over the contract term

The SSP of the SaaS platform alone is $195,000 (bundle discount applied). The SSP of onboarding is $30,000. TechCo has a 3-year history of achieving the uptime target 80% of the time.

### Five-step analysis

**Step 1 — Contract exists?**
All criteria met. Written agreement, identified rights, payment terms, commercial substance, and collectibility assessed as probable.

**Step 2 — Performance obligations**

| Promised service | Capable of being distinct? | Distinct in contract? | PO? |
|---|---|---|---|
| SaaS platform access | Yes | Yes | Separate PO (over time) |
| Onboarding / data migration | Yes — generic skills | Yes — separable | Separate PO (point in time) |

Two performance obligations identified.

**Step 3 — Transaction price**

- Base: $192,000
- VC (bonus): $24,000 × 80% EV = $19,200. Constraint check: history supports 80% achievement; uptime largely within TechCo's control. Include $19,200 (no constraint applied).
- **Total TP: $211,200**

**Step 4 — Allocate**

| PO | SSP | SSP % | Allocated TP |
|---|---|---|---|
| SaaS platform | $195,000 | 86.7% | $183,110 |
| Onboarding | $30,000 | 13.3% | $28,090 |
| **Total** | **$225,000** | **100%** | **$211,200** |

**Step 5 — Recognize**

- **Onboarding ($28,090):** Recognized in month 1 when the service is complete (point in time).
- **SaaS platform ($183,110):** Recognized over 24 months = $7,630/month (over time — simultaneous receipt and consumption).
- **VC reassessment:** At each reporting date, reassess the $19,200 VC estimate. If achievement probability changes, record a catch-up adjustment.

### Key judgments

1. Onboarding is distinct — generic skills applicable beyond this platform. If it were deeply embedded integration, it might be bundled with SaaS.
2. The bonus VC is included without constraint because history is favorable and uptime is within TechCo's control.
3. The discount ($225,000 SSP vs. $211,200 TP) is allocated proportionally — no basis for allocating entirely to one PO.

## Case study 2 — Long-term construction contract (cost-to-cost method)

### Facts

BuildCo enters a 3-year contract to construct a custom processing facility for a government client. Contract price: $10,000,000. At contract inception, estimated total costs are $8,000,000.

At the end of Year 1, costs incurred are $2,200,000 and revised total cost estimate is $8,800,000 (cost overrun identified).

At the end of Year 2, cumulative costs are $6,600,000 and revised total cost estimate is $9,000,000 (further overrun).

The contract has no alternative use for BuildCo, and the government has an unconditional right to the work in progress. BuildCo has an enforceable right to payment for all work performed to date.

### Five-step analysis

**Step 1:** Contract exists — government contract with all criteria met.

**Step 2:** Single performance obligation — construction of an integrated facility. Work is highly interrelated; customer controls the asset as it is built (Criterion 2 for over-time).

**Step 3:** Fixed TP = $10,000,000 (no variable consideration).

**Step 4:** Single PO — full TP allocated to it.

**Step 5 — Revenue recognition (cost-to-cost)**

| Period | Costs incurred | Total est. cost | % Complete | Cumulative revenue | Revenue this period |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Year 1 | $2,200,000 | $8,800,000 | 25.0% | $2,500,000 | $2,500,000 |
| Year 2 | $6,600,000 | $9,000,000 | 73.3% | $7,333,333 | $4,833,333 |
| Year 3 | $9,000,000 | $9,000,000 | 100.0% | $10,000,000 | $2,666,667 |

*Year 1: $2.2M / $8.8M = 25.0% × $10M = $2,500,000*
*Year 2: $6.6M / $9.0M = 73.3% × $10M = $7,333,333 − $2,500,000 = $4,833,333*

### Key judgments

1. Over-time criterion 2 applies (government controls the asset as built) — and criterion 3 also applies (no alternative use + payment right).
2. Cost estimates must be updated at every reporting date — cost overruns reduce future revenue, not current-period revenue.
3. If total estimated costs exceed the contract price, a loss must be recognized immediately in full (loss contract accounting).

## Knowledge check — True / False

Test your understanding of ASC 606. Review your answers against the key below.

**1.** Revenue can be recognized before a contract meets all five criteria in Step 1, as long as the entity is substantially certain it will collect.

**2.** A good or service that is "capable of being distinct" but not "distinct within the contract" must be bundled with related promises.

**3.** Under the expected value method, an entity multiplies the most likely outcome by its probability.

**4.** Variable consideration must always be fully excluded from the transaction price until the uncertainty is resolved.

**5.** A warranty that covers accidental damage beyond normal product defects is an assurance-type warranty.

**6.** Revenue from a perpetual software license (right-to-use) is recognized at the point in time the license is granted to the customer.

**7.** The residual approach for estimating SSP can be used whenever it produces a reasonable estimate.

**8.** In a consignment arrangement, revenue is recognized when the product is delivered to the consignee.

---

## Answer key

| # | Answer | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **False** | All five criteria must be met. If any criterion is unmet — including collectibility — no revenue is recognized. Consideration received is a liability. |
| 2 | **True** | Both parts of the distinct test must be met. Failing either part requires bundling with related promises until a distinct unit is identified. |
| 3 | **False** | The expected value method multiplies **each** possible outcome by its probability and sums the results. The most-likely-amount method uses the single most probable outcome. |
| 4 | **False** | Variable consideration is included to the extent it is highly probable that a significant revenue reversal will not occur. It is not always fully excluded. |
| 5 | **False** | A warranty covering accidental damage goes **beyond** assuring the product meets specifications — it is a **service-type warranty** and a separate performance obligation. |
| 6 | **True** | A perpetual software license (right-to-use) is recognized at the point in time the license is granted, because the customer obtains control at that moment. |
| 7 | **False** | The residual approach is permitted only when the SSP is **highly variable** or **uncertain**. It cannot be used simply because it is convenient or produces a desired result. |
| 8 | **False** | In a consignment arrangement, revenue is recognized only when the **consignee sells to the end customer** (or another specified event transfers control). Delivery to the consignee does not transfer control. |
