# Module 9 — Disclosures

## Overview

ASC 606-10-50 requires extensive disclosures designed to help financial statement users understand the **nature, amount, timing, and uncertainty** of revenue and cash flows from contracts with customers.

Disclosure requirements apply to all entities applying ASC 606, though nonpublic entities have certain reduced requirements.

## Quantitative disclosures

### 1. Disaggregation of revenue (ASC 606-10-50-5)

Revenue must be disaggregated into categories that depict how economic factors affect the **nature, amount, timing, and uncertainty** of revenue.

The appropriate categories depend on the entity and may include:
- Type of good or service (product line)
- Geographic region
- Market or customer type
- Contract duration (short-term vs. long-term)
- Timing of transfer (over time vs. point in time)
- Sales channel (direct vs. reseller)

:::note No prescribed format
ASC 606 does not mandate a specific disaggregation table. Choose the presentation that best explains your revenue story to users.
:::

### 2. Contract balances (ASC 606-10-50-8)

Disclose the opening and closing balances of:

| Balance | Description | Location |
|---|---|---|
| **Receivables** | Unconditional right to consideration | Balance sheet (A/R) |
| **Contract assets** | Revenue earned; not yet an unconditional right (e.g., unbilled) | Balance sheet (current or non-current) |
| **Contract liabilities** | Customer paid or has unconditional right to payment; entity has not yet performed | Balance sheet (deferred revenue) |

Also disclose:
- Revenue recognized from contract liabilities at the beginning of the period
- Revenue recognized from performance obligations satisfied (or partially satisfied) in prior periods

### 3. Remaining performance obligations (ASC 606-10-50-13)

Disclose the aggregate transaction price allocated to **unsatisfied (or partially unsatisfied) performance obligations** and when the entity expects to recognize that revenue.

Practical expedients available (see table below):
- Remaining PO disclosure not required for contracts with original expected duration of ≤1 year
- Remaining PO disclosure not required for amounts recognized using the right-to-invoice practical expedient

### 4. Contract cost assets (ASC 340-40-50)

Disclose:
- Closing balances of capitalized contract costs by asset category
- Amortization method
- Amortization recognized in the period
- Impairment losses recognized

## Qualitative disclosures

### 1. Significant judgments (ASC 606-10-50-17)

Disclose judgments that significantly affect the determination of the amount and timing of revenue, including:

- When performance obligations are satisfied (over time or at a point in time)
- For over-time POs: the method used to measure progress and why it is appropriate
- Significant judgments in determining the transaction price (e.g., constraint determination)
- Methods used to allocate the transaction price (SSP estimation methods)

### 2. Variable consideration (ASC 606-10-50-20)

Disclose:
- Types of variable consideration in the contracts
- Estimation method used (EV or MLA)
- Basis for applying the constraint
- Nature of any amounts not yet included due to constraint

### 3. Practical expedients elected (ASC 606-10-50)

Disclose which practical expedients have been elected and why.

## All practical expedients — reference table

| Expedient | Codification | What it permits |
|---|---|---|
| **Portfolio approach** | ASC 606-10-10-4 | Apply ASC 606 to a portfolio of contracts with similar characteristics instead of individual contracts |
| **Significant financing — ≤1 year** | ASC 606-10-32-18 | No adjustment for financing component when ≤12 months between payment and performance |
| **Sales tax exclusion** | ASC 606-10-32-2A | Exclude sales taxes from the transaction price on a net basis |
| **Shipping and handling — after control** | ASC 606-10-25-18B | Account for shipping/handling after control transfers as a fulfillment activity (not a separate PO) |
| **Right-to-invoice** | ASC 606-10-55-18 | Recognize revenue equal to the invoice amount if that directly corresponds to value delivered |
| **Remaining PO — ≤1 year** | ASC 606-10-50-14 | No disclosure of remaining POs for contracts with original duration ≤1 year |
| **Remaining PO — right-to-invoice** | ASC 606-10-50-14 | No disclosure of remaining POs when right-to-invoice expedient applies |
| **Contract cost — ≤1 year** | ASC 340-40-25-4 | Expense costs to obtain a contract when amortization period would be ≤1 year |
| **Modified retrospective — not restating** | ASC 606-10-65-1 | Adopt ASC 606 using cumulative catch-up (no restatement of prior years) |
| **Completed contracts — full retrospective** | ASC 606-10-65-1 | On full retrospective adoption, not required to restate completed contracts |

## Nonpublic entity reduced disclosures

Nonpublic entities are not required to provide:
- Disaggregation of revenue
- Remaining performance obligation quantitative disclosures
- Certain qualitative disclosures about significant judgments

However, they must still disclose:
- Qualitative description of performance obligations
- Contract balance disclosures
- Information about remaining POs if they elect not to use the right-to-invoice or ≤1-year expedients

## Key takeaways

- Disclosures cover four dimensions: nature, amount, timing, and uncertainty of revenue and cash flows
- Disaggregation format is flexible — choose categories that best explain economic drivers
- Contract balances (receivables, contract assets, contract liabilities) must be disclosed with opening/closing balances
- Remaining PO disclosure shows the revenue backlog — practical expedients can reduce this burden for short-term or right-to-invoice contracts
- Qualitative disclosures on significant judgments — especially constraint determinations and SSP methods — are often more informative than quantitative tables
- Elected practical expedients must be disclosed — auditors and investors will ask
