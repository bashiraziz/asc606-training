# Compliant scenarios

Five scenarios illustrating correct revenue recognition under ASC 606.

---

## Scenario 1 — SaaS subscription + onboarding

:::info Facts
**Customer:** Enterprise B2B client  
**Contract:** 24-month SaaS license ($8,000/month) + one-time onboarding service ($25,000)  
**Total contract value:** $217,000  
**SSPs:** SaaS platform $195,000 · Onboarding $30,000
:::

| Step | Analysis |
|---|---|
| Step 1 | Contract signed; all five criteria met. |
| Step 2 | Two POs: SaaS platform (over time) and onboarding (point in time). Onboarding uses generic implementation skills — capable of being distinct and separately identifiable. |
| Step 3 | TP = $217,000 (fixed; no VC). |
| Step 4 | SSP ratio: SaaS 86.7% / Onboarding 13.3%. Allocated: SaaS $188,139 · Onboarding $28,861. |
| Step 5 | Onboarding recognized at completion (month 1). SaaS recognized ratably over 24 months ($7,839/month). |

:::tip Why this is compliant
Two distinct POs are correctly identified and separated. The transaction price is allocated proportionally to relative SSPs — the bundle discount is not front-loaded into the onboarding fee to accelerate revenue.
:::

## Scenario 2 — Long-term construction contract

:::info Facts
**Customer:** Government agency  
**Contract:** 3-year design-build for a processing facility; fixed price $10,000,000  
**Total estimated costs at inception:** $8,000,000 (20% margin)  
**Progress method:** Cost-to-cost (input method)
:::

| Step | Analysis |
|---|---|
| Step 1 | Government contract; all criteria met; enforceable and in writing. |
| Step 2 | Single PO — integrated facility. Work is highly interrelated; no separable deliverables. |
| Step 3 | Fixed TP = $10,000,000. No variable consideration. |
| Step 4 | Single PO; full TP allocated. |
| Step 5 | Over time — Criterion 2 (customer controls asset as built) and Criterion 3 (no alternative use; payment right). Revenue = % complete × TP − prior cumulative. |

:::tip Why this is compliant
Over-time criteria correctly applied. Cost-to-cost progress is updated at every reporting date. Cost overruns reduce future revenue through revised % complete — they do not require restatement of prior periods.
:::

## Scenario 3 — Perpetual software license + post-contract support (PCS)

:::info Facts
**Customer:** Mid-market manufacturer  
**Contract:** Perpetual software license ($80,000) + 3-year PCS (support and upgrades; $12,000/year)  
**Total contract value:** $116,000  
**SSPs:** License $85,000 · PCS $15,000/year = $45,000
:::

| Step | Analysis |
|---|---|
| Step 1 | Written agreement; all criteria met. |
| Step 2 | Two POs: perpetual license (right to use) and PCS. PCS is separately sold and provides ongoing bug fixes and minor updates. |
| Step 3 | TP = $116,000. No VC. Financing component: customer pays in full at signing — gap is &lt;1 year for the license but PCS spans 3 years. Financing component on PCS assessed as not significant. |
| Step 4 | SSPs: License $85,000 (65.4%) · PCS $45,000 (34.6%). Allocated: License $75,780 · PCS $40,220. |
| Step 5 | License recognized at delivery (point in time — right-to-use). PCS recognized ratably over 3 years ($13,407/year). |

:::tip Why this is compliant
The license is correctly classified as right-to-use (static software; entity does not maintain the IP on an ongoing basis relevant to this customer). Discount allocated proportionally — not front-loaded to license.
:::

## Scenario 4 — Franchise fee + sales-based royalties

:::info Facts
**Customer:** New franchise operator  
**Contract:** Initial franchise fee $50,000 + 4% ongoing royalty on monthly gross sales  
**Services:** Initial setup, territory rights, brand license, ongoing marketing support
:::

| Step | Analysis |
|---|---|
| Step 1 | Franchise agreement; all criteria met. |
| Step 2 | Two POs: (1) initial setup services + territory rights (right to access the brand IP as the franchisor develops it) recognized over the franchise term; (2) ongoing marketing support services. |
| Step 3 | Fixed TP = $50,000 + variable royalty. Royalty is sales-based — exception applies (ASC 606-10-55-65). |
| Step 4 | Allocate $50,000 to the access license + setup bundle. Marketing support allocated from the royalty stream. |
| Step 5 | Initial fee recognized over franchise term (right-to-access the brand). Royalties recognized as the franchisee's sales occur — not earlier. |

:::tip Why this is compliant
The sales-based royalty exception correctly prevents recognizing royalty revenue before the underlying sales occur. The initial fee is spread over the franchise term because the franchise IP is right-to-access (franchisor actively maintains the brand).
:::

## Scenario 5 — Consignment arrangement

:::info Facts
**Customer:** Regional distributor network  
**Arrangement:** Manufacturer places $500,000 of finished goods with distributors; goods remain on manufacturer's books until sold to end customers. Unsold goods may be returned to manufacturer.
:::

| Step | Analysis |
|---|---|
| Step 1 | Consignment agreement executed; but note that no revenue contract exists until end-customer sale. |
| Step 2 | Single PO — delivery of goods to end customer. |
| Step 3 | TP determined at the time of end-customer sale. |
| Step 4 | Single PO. |
| Step 5 | **No revenue recognized on consignment delivery.** Inventory remains on manufacturer's balance sheet. Revenue recognized when distributor reports end-customer sale. |

:::tip Why this is compliant
Placing goods on consignment does not transfer control — the manufacturer can demand return and the distributor has no unconditional obligation to pay. Inventory is not derecognized until control transfers at end-customer sale.
:::

## Compliant indicators — quick reference

| Indicator | What it shows |
|---|---|
| Distinct POs correctly separated | No bundling or splitting for revenue manipulation |
| SSP-based allocation | No front-loading of revenue into early-delivery elements |
| VC constrained to highly probable amount | Conservative, evidence-based variable revenue estimates |
| Right-to-use recognized at delivery; right-to-access spread | License classification based on IP economics, not cash |
| Over-time criteria documented | Enforceable right to payment + no alternative use clearly evidenced |
| Consignment deferred to end-customer sale | Inventory control properly evaluated |
| Royalties recognized when sales occur | Sales-based royalty exception applied |
